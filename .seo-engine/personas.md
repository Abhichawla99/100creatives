# Persona Library — 100 Creatives Article Targeting

**Rule: One article = one persona.** Never write a generic "we help all D2C brands" article. The reader of every article should be able to recognise themselves in the first three sentences. The persona drives the angle, the search query, the FAQ wording, and the case-study reference.

**Anti-repetition:** Engine MUST NOT use the same persona twice within a rolling 30-day window. Check `MEMORY.md` before picking.

## P01 — The Apparel Founder Drowning in Drop Cadence
- **Who:** Founder/co-founder of an apparel/fashion DTC brand, $2M–$15M ARR, mostly Shopify, occasionally wholesale.
- **Catalog:** 200–2,000 SKUs, weekly or bi-weekly drops, often international model bookings.
- **Pain:** Photoshoots are bottleneck #1. Drops slip. Hero images inconsistent across collections. Studio bills crossed $30k/month and the founder is doing math on freelancers vs in-house studio vs AI.
- **Trigger to search:** Just blew a launch date because shoot day got pushed. Or just got a $45k photo invoice.
- **Search queries they type:** "AI fashion photography vs traditional", "how to scale apparel photoshoots", "replace photoshoots with AI", "AI photography for fashion brands cost".
- **Reference assets:** Anita Dongre case, ai-fashion-photography.html, dtc-clothing-brand-photography-playbook.html.

## How the engine uses this file

1. **Read MEMORY.md.** Identify the last 30 personas used (or all if <30 articles exist).
2. **Pick a persona NOT in that list.** If all 15 are in the recent list, find the persona with the oldest last-use date.
3. **Match topic to persona.** Each topic in `topics.json` has a `persona` field — that's the default. If memory says use a different persona, override and re-frame the article angle to that persona.
4. **Write the article TO that persona.** Open with their pain in their language. Reference the brand examples that mirror them. Use their search query as the FAQ #1.
5. **Log the persona in MEMORY.md.** Append the persona ID to today's entry.
