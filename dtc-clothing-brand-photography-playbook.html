The DTC photography math

Volume is 15-25 assets per SKU — every week

DTC apparel brands need dramatically more imagery per SKU than retail or wholesale equivalents. Performance-driven paid social requires weekly creative refresh. Email and retention flows each need dedicated imagery. PDP needs full variant coverage. Retargeting needs product-matched assets. The total pack per SKU runs 15 to 25 images; weekly refresh adds 2-3 more per month on active paid social SKUs.

A brand at 500 SKUs on full funnel photography needs 7,500 to 12,500 images monthly. At 2,000 SKUs the number runs 30,000 to 50,000. Traditional shoot throughput caps production at a fraction of this volume. The math stops working above $10M run rate; most DTC apparel brands either ship undercover assets, pay a fortune, or outsource offshore at quality risk.

AI production aligns structurally with DTC volume needs. Asset packs per SKU scale with brand ambition, not shoot day count. Full funnel coverage becomes a standard deliverable rather than a stretch goal. Brands moving from traditional to AI typically see their paid social efficiency lift 20-40 percent within one quarter because creative refresh cadence finally matches platform learning rhythm. Banner and module imagery. Product-family compositions. Wider aspect ratios for hero placements.

Wholesale carve-out

Brand-first imagery for wholesale and press when needed. Parallel pack from same reference. Not funnel-tuned.

The commercial shape

How DTC brands actually pay for full funnel

Most DTC apparel brands run AI photography on monthly retainer with volume commitment. Typical range: $10k-$50k per month depending on SKU count and paid social intensity. Brands at $5M-$10M run rate land in the $10k-$20k band. $10M-$30M brands land in $20k-$35k. Above $30M, pricing tends toward $35k-$50k or custom structures with dedicated production capacity.

The usual comparison is against combined traditional spend: photography retainer or shoots plus creative agency fees plus freelance creative direction. A typical DTC brand at $20M run rate spends $800k-$2M annually across those line items. AI production at the same volume runs $240k-$420k annually — a 60-80 percent reduction with more assets, faster delivery, and better paid social results.

Retainers scale with drop cadence, not with fixed annual commits. Brands compressing into holiday push typically double monthly volume for Q4, drop back to baseline in Q1. Brands running continuous drops stay at steady volume year-round. Flexibility is built into the commercial shape because DTC photography demand is never constant. See reduce costs.
