The economics

The math of traditional shoots no longer fits a DTC catalog

A single day of in-person product photography costs between $3,000 and $15,000 once you factor in studio rental, model day rate, stylist, photographer, assistant, and post-production. That day produces 15 to 25 usable looks. For a DTC brand with a 100-SKU catalog and weekly drops, you are looking at four to seven shoot days per season just for product coverage — call it $20,000 to $100,000 per season before a single ad creative is produced.

AI product photography, done at our standard, delivers the same catalog coverage at a fraction of that cost and arrives in 48 hours rather than four to six weeks. No studio booking. No model casting across two time zones. No weather-dependent outdoor sessions. No reshoot fees when a colorway behaves unexpectedly under studio lighting. Every asset is produced digitally with precise control over every variable, and colorway variants are same-day rather than a new shoot day.

The bigger unlock for DTC brands is not cost savings. It is velocity. The brands winning right now are the ones iterating creative faster than competitors. When photography takes 48 hours instead of six weeks, you can test three product positionings on the same SKU in the same week. You can launch the same product with different aesthetic treatments for different audience segments. You can respond to what your ad account is telling you in the same week instead of next quarter. See the full AI photoshoot vs studio cost breakdown for the math.

The brands that have already made the switch are not going back. The volume and velocity DTC requires today did not exist when the traditional shoot model was designed. AI product photography is what catalogs at this scale actually look like.
