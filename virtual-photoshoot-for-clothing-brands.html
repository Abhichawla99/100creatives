What it is

What a virtual photoshoot
actually is

A virtual photoshoot is a production workflow that replaces studio time, model booking, and location logistics with a digital pipeline that produces the same output categories — on-model hero imagery, flat lays, lifestyle context, detail shots, colorway variants. The garment is still real. The model is either a real person whose likeness you have rights to, or a generated identity you lock for your brand. The final images are pixel-accurate to the physical product.

What a virtual photoshoot is not: a consumer AI tool spitting out passable social media graphics. The difference between a production-grade virtual photoshoot and a free AI fashion image generator is the same as the difference between a commercial photography studio and a smartphone. Both produce images. Only one meets the bar for an ecommerce PDP, a paid social ad, or a wholesale catalog.

We have produced thousands of assets for apparel brands you would recognize — though under NDA we cannot name them. The through-line of every engagement is this: production discipline, not tool selection, is what makes AI fashion photography work at a professional standard. The same reason a $200 camera in a professional's hands beats a $20,000 camera in an amateur's hands applies here.

The workflow

Onboard once. Ship every week.

Day zero is a one-hour onboarding. You walk us through your brand kit, model preferences or reference photos of current models, color library, visual language examples, and export specifications. We build a brand profile that locks your aesthetic for every future production.

From that point on, the workflow is mechanical. You send garments — physical or high-resolution reference imagery — along with any specific creative direction for the drop. Within 48 hours we return first-pass assets on a shared review board. You leave precise revision notes inline. Revision passes take 24 hours. Final delivery is typically 72 hours from original brief for standard scope.

For brands with ongoing catalog needs, we run weekly or bi-weekly production windows. You queue garments across the week, we produce in batches, and you receive a complete weekly drop of finished assets on a consistent cadence. This is what on-model photography at scale looks like operationally — not one-off shoots, but an industrial pipeline that matches how your catalog actually moves.

Why switch

Weeks to 48 hours. Five figures to a fraction.

A real shoot costs $3,000 to $15,000 per day loaded, produces 15 to 25 looks, and takes two to six weeks from first brief to final retouched files. Virtual photoshoots produce comparable output in 48 hours at a fraction of that cost. The cost savings compound when you account for reshoots, colorway additions, and the opportunity cost of slow turnaround on trending styles.

The bigger unlock is flexibility. Need the same jacket on three different model types to test audience response? Done in the same cycle, no reshoot. Want a campaign set in Paris, Tokyo, and Los Angeles without the travel budget? Same brief, same 48-hour window. Launched a product last month and now the colorway you added is selling — need PDP imagery for it today? Same-day turnaround, not next quarter.

This is the operational reality brands mean when they talk about replacing photoshoots with AI. It is not a cost-cutting move — it is a velocity move. Brands winning in apparel right now are iterating on creative faster than their competitors, and photography speed is the constraint that has to break first.

Frequently asked
questions

What exactly is included in a virtual photoshoot?

A full virtual photoshoot delivers on-model hero shots, flat lay product shots, lifestyle context imagery, detail close-ups of fabric and construction, and ad-ready crops in every aspect ratio your media team needs. For a single SKU this typically means 8 to 24 final images covering PDP, social, and ad use cases. For a full catalog drop we scale throughput to match SKU count while maintaining one consistent visual language.

How does this compare to a real photoshoot?

Same output categories — on-model, flat lay, lifestyle, detail — without studio rental, model booking, stylist fees, location scouting, or post-production delays. Traditional shoots cap at 15 to 25 looks per day at $3,000 to $15,000 per day; virtual photoshoots produce comparable output in 48 hours at a fraction of the cost. Quality is production-grade, not consumer-grade AI.

Do I send you actual garments or just photos?

Either. Physical garments give tightest accuracy. High-resolution product images with multiple angles work for the majority of projects. We recommend physical garments for launch products and reference imagery for colorway variants of already-produced styles.

Can I use my brand's existing models?

Yes. Send us photographs of your current models and we produce new imagery with their likenesses wearing your garments. Model rights and licensing are handled on your side — we produce under whatever agreement structure you already have.

Can I request specific locations and settings?

Yes. Urban morning coffee shops, weekend travel, beach golden hour, rooftop evenings, studio white seamless — any setting briefable. Location scouting, weather, and travel disappear. A campaign across Paris, Tokyo, and LA can be produced in a single 48-hour cycle.

How do I approve and request revisions?

First-pass delivery in 48 hours on a shared Figma or Frame.io board. You leave precise revision notes inline. Revision passes take 24 hours. Final delivery typically within 72 hours of original brief for standard scope. Revision rounds are unlimited within scope.

What file formats and resolutions do I receive?

Print-ready (up to 4K long edge) PNG and JPG. Ad-format crops 1:1, 4:5, 9:16, 16:9, 1.91:1. Transparent background variants for PDPs. Custom Shopify, Amazon A+, PIM, email, and retargeting specs handled in standard workflow.

Is this suitable for luxury or premium apparel brands?

Yes. Luxury has the tightest quality bar, which is exactly why our production discipline matters most there. Cashmere, silk, fine knits, structured tailoring, premium leather — we hold luxury work to the same pass-fail standard as everything else. Premium brands we partner with use this output on PDPs, in campaigns, and in paid social without distinguishing it from traditional studio work.
