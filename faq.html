DTC Ad Strategy

How many ad creatives do I need per month for Meta?

Most DTC brands running Meta ads at scale need 30-100+ new static creatives per month to sustain effective testing and avoid ad fatigue. The exact number depends on your ad spend, the number of campaigns you're running, and how quickly your audience burns through creatives. Brands spending $100K+/month on Meta typically need the higher end of that range. The key is consistent creative volume — your best-performing ads will eventually fatigue, and you always need fresh angles in the pipeline. That's exactly what 100 Creatives is built to deliver.

Do static ads still work on Facebook and Instagram?

Yes — static ads are still one of the highest-performing ad formats on Meta. While video gets a lot of attention, static image ads consistently deliver strong ROAS for DTC brands because they communicate value propositions instantly, load faster, and are cheaper to produce at volume. Many of the top-spending DTC brands on Meta run a mix of static and video, with statics often outperforming video on cost-per-acquisition. The key is having well-designed statics with strong hooks, clear offers, and scroll-stopping visuals — which is our entire specialty.

What's the difference between a creative agency and a media buying agency?

A creative agency (like 100 Creatives) designs the actual ad assets — the images, graphics, and visuals that appear in your ads. A media buying agency manages your ad accounts — setting up campaigns, managing budgets, optimizing targeting, and analyzing performance data. They're complementary services, and most DTC brands need both. The problem is that many media buyers lack access to high-quality creative, which bottlenecks their ability to test and scale. That's exactly the gap we fill — we're the creative engine that feeds your media buyer's testing machine.

What makes a good static ad for ecommerce?

A high-converting static ad for ecommerce combines four elements: a scroll-stopping visual hook, a clear and compelling value proposition, social proof or credibility signals, and a strong call to action. The best static ads don't look like ads — they feel native to the platform and speak directly to the customer's pain points or desires. Product photography quality matters enormously, as does typography hierarchy and whitespace. At 100 Creatives, every ad we design is built on direct-response principles refined across thousands of creatives for DTC brands.

How do I scale my ad creatives without losing quality?

The biggest challenge in scaling creative output is maintaining quality and brand consistency as volume increases. The solution is working with a dedicated creative partner who deeply understands your brand — not a rotating cast of freelancers or an agency that assigns junior designers. At 100 Creatives, you get a dedicated senior designer who builds deep context about your brand over time, combined with our internal quality systems that ensure every creative meets our standards. This lets you scale to 100+ creatives per month without sacrificing the craft that makes ads convert. See how we maintain quality at scale in our Barefoot Wines project.

Should I hire an in-house designer or use an agency?

It depends on your stage and volume needs. A full-time in-house designer costs $60K-$120K+ per year in salary alone, plus benefits, tools, management overhead, and the risk of a single point of failure if they leave or burn out. A performance creative agency like 100 Creatives gives you access to senior-level talent at a fraction of the cost, with faster turnarounds and no HR headaches. For most DTC brands doing $5M-$50M/year, a dedicated creative agency is the smarter move — you get agency-level output without the fixed overhead, and you can scale up or down based on seasonal demand.
