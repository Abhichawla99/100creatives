FAQ for CPG marketing & brand teams

The questions CPG buyers ask us.

What does a CPG creative agency actually deliver?
Every brief a CPG brand throws — hero brand campaigns, OOH and hero banners, retail and point-of-sale, UGC and creator content, social-first video, performance ad creative, and product photography at scale. One full-stack creative and content team instead of four separate vendors.
How do you handle both brand and performance creative under one roof?
Senior creative directors hold the brand line. The performance team works downstream of those guidelines, so the static and video ads your media buyer is testing on Meta and TikTok still look like the same brand on the OOH unit and on shelf. One team, one system, one source of truth.
Do you work with CPG brands at retail — shelf wobblers, end-cap, OOH?
Yes. Retail and point-of-sale is one of our seven core services. Shelf wobblers, end-cap design, in-store posters, channel-partner toolkits, wholesale banners, and OOH key art for grocery, mass, club, and specialty channels.
Which CPG categories do you specialize in?
Beverage (alcohol, non-alcoholic, soda, functional), snack and food, supplement and wellness, beauty and skincare, and household and home care. If it lives on a shelf and a feed, we ship the creative for it.
How fast can you ship CPG creative?
Studio velocity, brand-led craft. Static ad batches and social cuts ship inside the week. UGC drops follow a weekly cadence. Hero campaigns and OOH key art run on a campaign timeline, not a single sprint. Revisions are answered inside 48 hours.
What is the engagement model and what does it cost?
Flat monthly retainer with a named senior team. No quote-by-quote chaos, no surprise invoices. CPG brands pick a tier based on monthly volume across the seven services and we ship to that cadence every month.
Do you work with CPG brands outside the US?
Yes. We are based in Calgary and ship creative for CPG brands across North America, the UK, Europe, and APAC. The retainer model and SLAs are the same regardless of timezone.
Will the same team work on every brief, or do you rotate juniors?
Same named senior team, every month. That is the entire point of a retainer with us — the brand fluency compounds, the creative gets sharper, and the work does not drift off-brand by month two.
