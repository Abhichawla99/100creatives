The juggling act we replaced

Most supplement CPG brands juggle four vendors to ship a season

A brand agency for the hero campaign. A UGC shop for social. A banner studio for retail and OOH. A freelancer for performance ads. Four contracts, four interpretations of the brand guidelines, four sets of deadlines that never quite line up. By the time the season ships, the launch platform has drifted from the retail end-cap, the creator content feels like it belongs to a different brand, and the performance ads are fighting their own positioning in the auction.

Supplement and nutraceutical CPG brands feel this acutely. The retailer category review, the OOH campaign, the influencer drop, the Meta retargeting flow — the consumer touches all of them in the same week, and the visual story has to hold across every surface. When four vendors are stitching it together, it almost never does.

We replaced the juggling act. One named senior team, one flat monthly retainer, the full creative stack — built around a category that demands compliance, credibility, and craft on the same calendar.

What breaks when creative is split across vendors

  • Compliance drift. One vendor's structure-function language is another's prohibited claim. When work is split, claims slip through that get the whole account flagged.
  • Brand drift across surfaces. The hero OOH, the retail FSI, the Reel, and the retargeting ad start to look like four different brands — the consumer feels the dissonance.
  • Calendar drift. Retailer windows and category reviews do not move. Vendor calendars do. Splitting creative across four shops means something always lands late.
  • Cost drift. Four retainers, four onboarding cycles, four scope-creep arguments per quarter — the math stops working long before the season ships.

Case study

Our supplement CPG work:
ARMRA Colostrum

ARMRA is a physician-founded colostrum CPG brand that has defined the category — backed by decades of research on bovine colostrum and a brand spine built for both retailer shelves and the feed. The work spans hero brand creative, retail and on-pack, social-first content, and performance ad creative — all under one team, all on the same calendar. Six examples below.

Clinical authority This creative leads with ARMRA's physician-founded positioning and clean product photography against a clinical white backdrop. The restrained palette and precise typography signal pharmaceutical-grade quality, immediately separating ARMRA from the crowded supplement aisle.
Benefit-first messaging The headline speaks directly to the outcome the consumer is seeking. Rather than leading with the brand name or product description, this creative opens with the transformative benefit and lets the product imagery reinforce the claim. The visual hierarchy guides the eye from headline to product to CTA in a natural reading flow.
Social proof stack This creative layers multiple trust signals into a single frame: star ratings, review volume, and a highlighted customer quote. For an ingestible product, this density of social proof is critical. It transforms the ad from a brand message into a peer recommendation.
Science meets lifestyle This creative bridges the gap between clinical credibility and aspirational lifestyle. By pairing scientific callouts with warm, relatable imagery, it speaks to both the rational and emotional brain. The consumer sees a product that is both rigorously researched and naturally integrated into daily life.
Ingredient education Rather than asking the viewer to trust a generic "superfood" claim, this creative breaks down the specific nutrients and bioactive compounds in colostrum. The visual hierarchy makes complex science scannable in seconds. Educated consumers reward this transparency with higher engagement and conversion rates.
Credibility plus urgency This creative combines the trust-building elements from the other angles with a time-sensitive offer. The credibility foundation (physician-founded, research-backed, thousands of reviews) is established visually, while the CTA introduces urgency. This framework consistently delivers the lowest CPA in supplement ad accounts.
